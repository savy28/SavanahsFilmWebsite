About Me

About

Savanah is known for her captivating performances in several feature films including “A Weird Kind of Beautiful,” “Flight Risk,” and “Handshake”. Her talent and dedication have earned her the Best Actress award at multiple noted film festivals including Orlando Film Festival and Marina Del Rey. She recently finished a thriller that takes place in the obscure nightlife of Argentina, "Lying in Wait."

Born and raised in the small town of Yakima Washington, she developed a deep love for storytelling early on, recognizing it as an essential part of the human experience. Now based in Los Angeles, Savanah continues to explore and portray the stories of individuals from all walks of life with passion. Savanah strives to connect with audiences on a deeper level.

As a curious thinker of many interests, Savanah is now writing and directing alongside her unceased love for acting. She has written several shorts that have earned their way into festivals and won a range of awards, one called "Deadpan" as well as "Mom's Funeral" that explores the fragmented relationship between two sisters after their mother passes. She is currently writing a psychological horror, "The Laff Box."
